Gateway to Karnataka’s Ancient and Medieval Treasures
Koppal district, rich in history and natural beauty, showcases a diverse heritage with ancient temples, forts, and rocky landscapes. It’s an ideal base for exploring forgotten ruins, temples, and the famous Vijayanagara region.
Koppal’s famed Hampi ruins lie close by, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
The Anegundi region in Koppal is linked to the Ramayana epic and Vijayanagara history.
The Mahadeva Temple at Itagi (near Koppal) is hailed as a masterpiece of Western Chalukya architecture.
The district includes scenic hills and wildlife sanctuaries.
Koppal Fort overlooks the city with majestic views.
Explore rock-cut temples and historical forts.
Take guided tours to uncover Vijayanagara and Chalukya legacies.
Visit traditional villages for folk arts and crafts.
Birdwatch at Naradagadde Bird Sanctuary along the Tungabhadra River.
Enjoy regional cuisine in local eateries.
By Road: Approximately 365 km from Bengaluru via NH50, NH67; buses and taxis connect the city. By Rail: Koppal Railway Station with trains to major Karnataka cities and beyond. By Air: Hubballi Airport (145 km); Kempegowda International Airport Bengaluru (approx. 360 km).
Carry comfortable walking shoes for exploring ruins and hills.
Best months for travel are October to March due to pleasant weather.
Respect local customs and temple protocols.
Carry water and sun protection when trekking or sightseeing outdoors.
Plan combined visits with Hampi for a comprehensive cultural experience.
