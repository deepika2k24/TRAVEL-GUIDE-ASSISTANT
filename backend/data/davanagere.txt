Located in the geographical center of Karnataka, Davanagere is a city that delights the palate and tells the story of the state’s textile heritage. You can visit for its legendary Benne Dose and to experience the soul of a true central Karnataka trading town.
Davanagere is the birthplace of the world-famous Davanagere Benne Dose, a buttery, crispy dosa that has a cult following.
The city was once known as the “Manchester of Karnataka” for its flourishing cotton textile mills.
Shantisagara, located nearby, is one of Asia’s largest irrigation tanks, built in the 12th century.
Kunduvada Kere: A picturesque lake on the city’s outskirts, perfect for evening strolls, birdwatching, and enjoying the sunset.
Sri Anjaneya Swamy Temple: A prominent and revered temple in the city dedicated to Lord Hanuman, attracting devotees from all over the region.
The Glass House: A unique attraction located within a garden, this structure is used for horticultural shows and public events.
Harihar: A nearby town on the banks of the Tungabhadra river, famous for the ancient and beautiful Harihareshwara Temple.
Indulge in the authentic Davanagere Benne Dose at one of the city’s iconic, old-school eateries.
Enjoy a relaxing evening at Kunduvada Kere, a popular local spot for recreation and unwinding amidst nature.
Explore the local markets to get a feel for the city’s commercial legacy and shop for textiles.
Take a short trip to the Shantisagara reservoir for a picnic and to admire the vast expanse of water.
By Air: The nearest airports are in Hubballi (HBX), around 143 km away, and Bengaluru (BLR), around 260 km away.
By Rail: Davanagere is a major railway junction on the Bengaluru-Hubballi line, with excellent connectivity.
By Road: Situated on National Highway 48, Davanagere is very well-connected by road to all major cities in Karnataka.
Modern business hotels catering to commercial travelers.
Comfortable lodges and guesthouses suitable for families.
Highway-side inns offering convenient stopover options.
Trying the Benne Dose is a must-do, also ask locals for recommendations on the best places.
As a central hub, Davanagere makes for an excellent base to explore the surrounding regions of Chitradurga and Shivamogga.
The city experiences hot summers, so the best time to visit is during the cooler months from October to February.
