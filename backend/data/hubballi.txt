Northern Karnataka’s buzzing business hub
Hubballi, paired often with its twin city Dharwad, is a bustling commercial hub in Karnataka known for its blend of industry and culture. Visitors come for eclectic markets, historic temples, and the gateway to North Karnataka’s heritage sites.
Hubballi-Dharwad is the second largest urban agglomeration in Karnataka after Bengaluru.
The city hosts Karnataka’s oldest public library and one of the largest railway junctions in India.
Hubballi has a famous Rani Chennamma Circle, celebrating a warrior queen revered in Karnataka.
It is known for a vibrant Kannada theater and music culture.
Hubli Fort and Dargah of Hazrat Khalil Ullah
Explore local markets for textiles, perfumes, and handicrafts.
Attend cultural performances and music festivals.
Visit cafés and restaurants serving North Karnataka cuisine.
Take day trips to historical sites like Badami and Pattadakal.
By Road: Well connected by NH63 and NH52, 410 km from Bengaluru. By Rail: Hubballi Junction Railway Station, a major rail hub. By Air: Hubballi Airport, with daily flights to major cities.
Summers (April–June) can be hot; best to visit in cooler months (Oct–March).
Local transport is readily available but plan connections to nearby attractions.
Kannada is the primary language; English is widely understood.
Always confirm transport schedules in advance.
Dress modestly at temples and religious sites.
