If you’re searching for a clean, peaceful and eco-friendly beach in Karnataka, Kasarkod Beach should be at the top of your list. Located in the coastal district of Uttara Kannada, Kasarkod is proudly known as India’s first Blue Flag–certified beach, setting a benchmark for sustainable tourism and responsible travel.
Unlike crowded tourist beaches, Kasarkod offers a serene coastline, pristine sands, and well-maintained facilities – making it perfect for families, solo travelers, and nature lovers alike.
Kasarkod Beach is situated near Honnavar, along the Arabian Sea coast of Karnataka. The beach gained national recognition after receiving the Blue Flag certification, an international eco-label awarded to beaches that meet high standards of cleanliness, safety, environmental management, and accessibility.
What truly sets Kasarkod apart is its balance between natural beauty and modern infrastructure, ensuring visitors enjoy comfort without harming the ecosystem.
Why Kasarkod Beach is Special (Blue Flag Standards)
Kasarkod Beach meets strict Blue Flag parameters, including:
Crystal-clean shoreline and water quality
Well-maintained restrooms and changing rooms
Waste management and eco-awareness signage
Dedicated zones for relaxation and recreation
These features make Kasarkod one of the best beaches in Karnataka for responsible tourism.
Kasarkod is well connected by road and can be easily reached from major towns in Karnataka.
Regular buses and private vehicles operate till Honnavar, from where Kasarkod Beach is just a short drive.
Nearest Railway Station: Honnavar Railway Station (approx. 6 km)
Honnavar is connected to major cities like Bengaluru, Mangaluru, and Mumbai via Konkan Railway.
Nearest Airport: Dabolim Airport, Goa (approx. 165 km)
From the airport, taxis and buses are available to reach Honnavar and Kasarkod.
The ideal time to visit Kasarkod Beach is from October to March.
Winter (Oct – Feb): Pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing and beach walks
Summer (Mar – May): Warm but manageable, fewer crowds
Monsoon (Jun – Sep): Lush surroundings, but swimming may be restricted due to rough seas
